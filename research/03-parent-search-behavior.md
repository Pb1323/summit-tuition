# Parent Search Behaviour: What UK Parents Actually Ask About 11+ Prep

*Research for Summit Tuition — compiled July 2026 from Mumsnet, the Eleven Plus Exams forum (elevenplusexams.co.uk), and related UK parenting communities. All quotes are verbatim (or near-verbatim as reported in thread summaries) with thread sources.*

---

## 1. The big themes

Across dozens of threads, parent conversation about the 11+ clusters into six recurring anxieties. In rough order of frequency:

1. **Do we actually need a tutor, or can we do this ourselves?** (the single most common question)
2. **When should we start?** — with real fear of being "too late" *and* social disapproval of starting "too early"
3. **How much will this cost?** — sticker shock at hourly rates and multi-year totals
4. **Are mock exams worth the money?**
5. **Is this damaging my child?** — stress, pressure, and the parent–child relationship
6. **Is the test really "tutor-proof"?** — cynicism about the arms race and about test providers' claims

---

## 2. Theme: Tutor vs DIY

The dominant framing is not "which tutor?" but "**do we really need one?**" — parents arrive sceptical and price-sensitive, then get talked into it by the competitive-arms-race argument.

**What parents say:**

- "A lot of applicants get into those schools without external tutors, i.e DIY" — [Do we really need tutor for 11+? (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/4825685-do-we-really-need-tutor-for-11)
- "Be under no illusion these kids are tutored big time!" — same thread
- "11+ exams have a tight time limit. Being taught exam technique is quite important" — same thread
- "self motivation and self study is no longer enough" because "everyone else does it" — same thread
- "If your own standard of English/Maths is very good AND you can explain things well" [you can DIY] — same thread
- DIY parents lean on **CGP, Bond, Atom Learning and BOFA**: "Atom is good but you need written practice papers too"; "Bond unhelpfully doesn't give answers, while Atom isn't especially challenging" — [11+ ISEB prep — Atom v Bond Online Plus (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/5255898-11-iseb-prep-atom-v-bond-online-plus), [11 plus preparation DIY route (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/primary/5164671-11-plus-preparation-diy-route)
- The consistent DIY caveat: "If you're not using a tutor, as a parent you'll have to do a certain amount of tutoring yourself" — you need to understand exam technique for the specific test — [11 plus online resources (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/4261043-11-plus-online-resources)

**Insight for Summit:** the market genuinely splits into DIY-with-platform parents and full-tutor parents. A hybrid ("structured platform + expert oversight + real mocks") speaks directly to the anxious middle who don't want £3k of tuition but don't trust themselves to know "how to tackle tricky questions and exam technique".

## 3. Theme: When to start

The most emotionally loaded question. Parents fear both lateness and over-tutoring, and threads titled like "[Have I left it too late to start 11+ prep?](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/5149796-have-i-left-it-too-late-to-start-11-prep)" are perennial.

**What parents say (all from the "left it too late" thread unless noted):**

- "Most kids start at the very beginning of yr 5 so you're fine. It's generally frowned upon to start earlier."
- "Most start September/October of year 5… So don't panic about being late. You are bang on time!"
- "Starting in year 4 is madness" (counter-view: "Most kids start 11 plus tutoring from Y4")
- "I don't believe it's good for a child to be pressured so young."
- "Some kids only do a short intensive prep in the lead up to the exam — which is fine if they are strong contenders."
- In hyper-competitive areas: "you may not find a tutor if you don't start by September of year 5" — [Mumsnet tutoring threads](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/4805675-ideal-time-to-get-an-11-tutor)
- Year 4 advice skews gentle: "little and often", 15–30 minutes a day, solid foundations, daily reading — [11plusguide.com Year 4 guide](https://www.11plusguide.com/11-plus-exam-preparation/general-advice/start/11-plus-year-4-preparation/), [Bond: when to start](https://www.bond11plus.co.uk/11-plus-when-to-start)

**Consensus:** start of Year 5 is the social norm; ~12 months before the test. Year 4 = foundations only. This maps cleanly onto "year-by-year timeline" content.

## 4. Theme: Cost

Parents openly benchmark prices against each other, and cost threads get heavy traffic.

**Reported price points:**

- London: "40–50 per hour is really common, and experienced ones charge 60–80" — [£45 to £60 per hour for 11 plus tuition (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/4880549-ps45-to-ps60-per-hour-for-11-plus-tuition)
- Midlands: ~£25/hr; Bucks/Kent/Essex £50–70/hr — [11 plus tutoring costs (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/3763836-11-plus-tutoring-costs), [Atom cost breakdown](https://www.atomlearning.com/blog/11-plus-tutoring-cost)
- Typical annual spend: ~£2,750 for weekly 1:1 over a year; Mumsnet threads "regularly feature parents describing bills of £5,000–8,000 over two years" — [To ask how much are you paying for 11 plus tuition? (Mumsnet AIBU)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/am_i_being_unreasonable/5201637-to-ask-how-much-are-you-paying-for-11-plus-tuition)
- Group option: "£35 for two hours" with 4 kids vs "£75" 1:1 in North London — [11 plus — 1 2 1 tuition or group tuition (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/4880259-11-plus-1-2-1-tuition-or-group-tuition)
- Value verdict when it works: "£160 per month… worth every penny if you find someone good" — [Mumsnet tutoring threads](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/tutoring/4914179-11-tutors-online)
- Oxford specifically: averages quoted at "£27/h" (Superprof) to "£32" (Tutorful) — see SEO file.

**Insight:** a premium online offer priced clearly *between* £10/mo platforms (Atom) and £50+/hr 1:1 tutors has an obvious cost-anchoring story parents already understand.

## 5. Theme: Mock exams

Mocks are broadly seen as worth it — but for **experience, not prediction**. This is the most directly relevant theme for Summit's mock product.

**What parents say (from [11+ mock exam waste of money or benefit (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/3413613-11-mock-exam-waste-of-money-or-benefit) unless noted):**

- "mocks are just as important as the prep. Difference between qualify or not especially if borderline"
- "more for the experience of being in a huge hall with hundreds of other children"
- "provided test conditions so that she had experience of what to do/when and how to cope"
- "useful for us to gauge how she was doing and for her to see the format/timing"
- "mock tests in the Feb and Spring helped focus on areas of improvement"
- "it was all about the experience" — and it improved confidence "when it came to the real thing"
- Recommended cadence: 2–3 mocks across Easter, June and late July of Year 5, across formats.

**The counter-arguments (objections to answer on a landing page):**

- Predictive validity doubts: "child failed every formal mock but passed the actual exam with points to spare" — [11+ mocks — any miracle stories?! (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/5339781-11-mocks-any-miracle-stories)
- Poorly pitched difficulty "damaged confidence more than it helped" — [Mock exam 11+… mock test results at this stage (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/5309550-mock-exam-11mock-test-results-at-this-stage)
- The cheap substitute: "easier, cheaper and less stressful to use home exam packs like CGP or Bond under exam conditions" — [11+ mock exam waste of money or benefit](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/3413613-11-mock-exam-waste-of-money-or-benefit)
- Panic threads after bad mocks are a genre of their own: "[11 plus Mock Disaster — what now](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/3297685-11-plus-Mock-Disaster-what-now)", "[11+ 3rd mock exam and no improvement](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/4607212-11-3rd-mock-exam-and-no-improvement)"

**Insight:** sell mocks on *exam-conditions experience + diagnostic feedback + confidence*, never on score prediction. Post-mock guidance ("what your score means at this stage") answers the single biggest post-mock anxiety.

## 6. Theme: Stress and the child

The emotional undertow of every thread. Parents police each other hard on this.

**What parents say:**

- "a grammar school isn't worth damaging your relationship with your child" — [Mumsnet tutoring threads](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/tutoring/4660568-11-tutoring)
- "A stressed child won't perform to their best level anyway." — [11plus pressure (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/Talk/education/a2071442-11plus-pressure)
- "Should I put my average child through 11+?" — recurring thread title in itself — [Mumsnet](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/4995670-should-i-put-my-average-child-through-11)
- "It's summer OP. Your child would not thank you for forcing him to work in the holidays." — [Have I left it too late…](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/education/5149796-have-i-left-it-too-late-to-start-11-prep)
- Practice norm cited: "practice papers for about 30 minutes a day" while "worrying about damaging their relationship with their child" — [11plus pressure](https://www.mumsnet.com/Talk/education/a2071442-11plus-pressure)
- "Over-tutoring" is a recognised pejorative with its own threads — [What is 11+ Over Tutoring? (Mumsnet)](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/tutoring/4701710-what-is-11-over-tutoring)

**Insight:** premium positioning must be *calm*, not hothouse. "Low-stress", "confidence", "no burnout", "little and often" are the phrases parents reward each other for using.

## 7. Theme: "Tutor-proof" scepticism and the arms race

Parents are openly cynical about test-provider claims — useful for honest, authority-building content.

- "CEM doesn't claim it is tutor proof but more resistant to the effects of tutoring, although there is no published evidence of this" — [CEM not tutor-proof (Eleven Plus Exams forum)](https://www.elevenplusexams.co.uk/forum/11plus/viewtopic.php?f=70&t=48268)
- "the main complaint about CEM is usually that it is in no way tutor proof and is far too much about speed" — [how does CEM differ from GL assessment 11+ exam? (Eleven Plus Exams forum)](https://www.elevenplusexams.co.uk/forum/11plus/viewtopic.php?t=40498)
- "her daughter said she didn't think anyone would be able to pass the test without tutoring" — [Tutors seem to have upped the ante (Eleven Plus Exams forum)](https://www.elevenplusexams.co.uk/forum/11plus/viewtopic.php?t=56097&start=30)
- Wondering "whether the high level of tutoring of other children meant that their able daughter didn't get a place" — [Over tutored pupils (Eleven Plus Exams forum)](https://www.elevenplusexams.co.uk/forum/11plus/viewtopic.php?t=41340)
- GL-specific: "The GL question bank reasoning types are seen as biased in favour of tutored children" — same forum cluster.

## 9. Note on channels

- **Mumsnet** dominates organic discussion volume (Talk boards: primary, secondary, education, tutoring, AIBU). Netmums has negligible visible 11+ discussion — searches for Netmums 11+ content return Mumsnet threads instead.
- **elevenplusexams.co.uk forum** is the specialist venue — region-specific subforums (e.g. Bucks, Berks, Oxford-adjacent counties) with deep technical detail on test formats. Note: it blocks scrapers (403), but is highly visible in search snippets.
- **Reddit** (r/UKParenting etc.) has no meaningful indexed 11+ presence — searches return nothing substantive. Not a channel worth targeting.
