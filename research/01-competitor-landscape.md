# Competitor Landscape — UK 11+ Tuition & Mock Exams

*Market research for Summit Tuition (premium pre-launch 11+ online tuition + GL-style mock platform, Oxford). Compiled July 2026. All prices as found at research date; verify before quoting externally.*

Context worth stating up front: **Oxfordshire itself has no state grammar schools** — its state secondaries are comprehensive ([11plustutoring.uk — Oxfordshire](https://www.11plustutoring.uk/11-plus-exams/county/11-plus-tutoring-oxfordshire/)). Local 11+ demand therefore splits between (a) **independent school entry** (Magdalen College School, Oxford High GDST, Abingdon, d'Overbroeck's — all named as target schools by local providers, e.g. [Explore Learning Oxford](https://www.explorelearning.co.uk/tuition-centre/oxford/)) and (b) families within reach of **Buckinghamshire, Reading and Slough grammar schools**, all of which use GL Assessment or GL-style tests. That dual market shapes who competes here.

---

## (a) Online-only 11+ platforms

### 1. Atom Learning — the category leader
- **Pricing:** £39.99/month (English, Maths & Science), £59.99/month (Exam Prep), £69.99/month (Exam Prep Plus); annual plans save ~20% ([Atom Learning pricing](https://www.atomlearning.com/pricing), figures via search snippet of that page).
- **Offer:** adaptive self-serve practice platform, online timed mock tests, exam-planning tools; also sells 1:1 "Atom Tuition" as an upsell ([Atom — 11+ practice papers](https://www.atomlearning.com/features/11-plus-practice-papers)).
- **Positioning:** data-driven, all-in-one 11+ prep; publishes content marketing like "How much does 11+ tutoring cost?" to position itself as the cheaper alternative to tutors ([Atom blog](https://www.atomlearning.com/blog/11-plus-tutoring-cost)).
- **Weaknesses (well-documented by parents):** volatile/dubious standardised (SAS) scoring — "you could score 130 one day, 110 the next with the same number wrong"; the ever-present timer and score tracker cause child stress; UI called "clunky" (scrolling between comprehension pages); content pitched too hard for some children who then get discouraged; at least one billing complaint of £100+/month charged for tuition not delivered ([Mumsnet threads](https://www.mumsnet.com/talk/secondary/3942268-Atom-Learning-useful-When-to-start), [Trustpilot reviews](https://www.trustpilot.com/review/atomlearning.co.uk)). It is also the **premium-priced** option in its category by a wide margin.

### 2. BOFA 11+ — the budget adaptive platform
- **Pricing:** £9.50/month rolling, no tie-in; fixed terms of 3/6/9/12 months; school-partner offers such as 6 months + 1 free for £40 and 12 months + 3 free for £70 ([BOFA subscriptions explained](https://www.bofa11plus.com/bofahelp/subscriptions-explained), [BOFA school offer](https://www.bofa11plus.com/content/offer-subscription)).
- **Offer:** self-serve "test–teach–retest" adaptive online testing; separate **mock test packs sold in threes** (ISEB pre-test, London 11+ Consortium, school-specific) ([BOFA help — subscription vs mocks](https://www.bofa11plus.com/bofahelp/difference-11plus-pretest)).
- **Positioning:** "Marked Online 11+ Tests | Bespoke Practice | Retests" — automation and value ([bofa11plus.com](https://www.bofa11plus.com/)).
- **Weaknesses:** utilitarian product and brand; parents on Mumsnet who compared it directly preferred Atom; no human layer, no invigilated mock experience, weak reporting story versus mock-day providers.

### 3. CGP 11+ Online — the books brand goes digital
- **Pricing:** monthly subscription (auto-renewing, cancel anytime) with a yearly plan saving 30%; exact figures not displayed in search results — treat as low-£/month, book-brand priced ([CGP 11+ Online subscriptions](https://www.cgpbooks.co.uk/11-plus-13-plus-books/11/11-plus-online-subscriptions)).
- **Offer:** "unlimited 11+ practice for every subject and test provider, with quick topic tests, full practice tests, and personalised" recommendations ([CGP Facebook launch copy](https://www.facebook.com/cgpbooks/videos/cgp-11-online-has-arrived/1335109570554167/)).
- **Positioning:** trusted budget publisher extending into digital; funnel starts from free practice papers ([CGP free 11+ tests](https://www.cgpbooks.co.uk/info/preparing-for-the-11-plus-with-cgp/free-11-plus-practice-tests)).
- **Weaknesses:** no tuition, no mocks-as-events, no benchmarking against a cohort; a practice bank, not a preparation programme.

### 4. Pretest Plus — school-specific online tests (independent-school skew)
- **Offer:** online practice tests replicating "the timings, formats and question styles your child can expect on exam day", built per target school — 60+ schools listed including Abingdon School (Oxford-relevant), Dulwich, Cheltenham Ladies' ([pretestplus.co.uk](https://pretestplus.co.uk/), [11+ category](https://pretestplus.co.uk/product-category/eleven-plus-exam/)).
- **Pricing:** per-test and bundle purchases; bundles discount 25–33%, with advertised savings of £61–£226 per bundle (implying full bundles run well into three figures, e.g. Cheltenham Ladies' 14-test bundle "save £226"); sitewide 10% off orders over £80 ([Pretest Plus bundle pages](https://pretestplus.co.uk/product/london-11-plus-consortium-practice-tests-bundle/)).
- **Positioning:** accuracy of replication for named-school exams (ISEB, CAT4, CEM Select, UKiset).
- **Weaknesses:** expensive at bundle level; one-attempt test packs rather than a learning platform; grammar-school GL coverage is secondary to its independent/pretest core.

### 5. CoolCleverKids — CEM-legacy long-tail
- **Offer:** online maths and reasoning practice for primary + 11+, historically CEM-oriented ([coolcleverkids.co.uk](https://www.coolcleverkids.co.uk/)).
- **Pricing:** not published in search results; sold via subscription (third-party listing at [SatsBooks](https://www.satsbooks.com/coolcleverkidscouk-subscription)).
- **Weaknesses:** dated site, CEM association is now legacy (CEM exited the traditional 11+ market and regions have migrated to GL/FSCE); low brand visibility versus Atom/BOFA. Illustrates how CEM-era players have not repositioned cleanly for the GL-dominant era.

## (b) Oxford / Oxfordshire local provision

### 6. Explore Learning Oxford — the only major physical centre chain in the city
- **Offer:** membership-based tuition centre "upstairs in Sainsbury's", ages 4–16, sessions ~1h15, once or twice weekly, English/maths plus 11+ and entrance-exam prep; names Abingdon, Magdalen College School and Oxford High as schools it has supported applications to ([Explore Learning Oxford](https://www.explorelearning.co.uk/tuition-centre/oxford/)).
- **Pricing:** 11+/entrance-exam memberships from ~£21 per session; specialised 11+ packages reported around £175/month (weekly) to £255/month (twice weekly), varying by location and in-centre vs online ([Explore Learning pricing](https://www.explorelearning.co.uk/pricing/), figures via [third-party review roundup](https://www.myengineeringbuddy.com/blog/explore-learning-reviews-alternatives-pricing-offerings/)).
- **Positioning:** "Award-winning tuition for ages 4–16" — friendly, mainstream, curriculum-first with 11+ bolted on.
- **Weaknesses:** generalist, not an 11+ specialist; group/rotational tutor model; no GL-style mock-exam-day product with cohort reports; supermarket-mezzanine setting is the opposite of premium Oxford positioning.

### 7. Marketplace tutors in Oxford (Superprof, Tutorfair, Tutorful, Tutor Hunt, Owl Tutors)
- **Pricing:** Superprof lists 11+ tutors in Oxford "from £15/hr", average ~£27/hr ([Superprof Oxford](https://www.superprof.co.uk/lessons/11-school-exam-preparation/oxford/)); Tutorfair average £32/hr for Oxford 11+ ([Tutorfair](https://www.tutorfair.com/11-plus-tutor-oxford)); premium agency tutors (Owl Tutors, who list 9 tutors with Magdalen College School prep experience) start around £70/hr ([Owl Tutors — MCS guide](https://owltutors.co.uk/schools/magdalen-college-school/), [Owl Tutors Oxford](https://owltutors.co.uk/uk/oxfordshire/tutors-in-oxford/)).
- **Positioning:** fragmented; quality signalled by individual tutor profiles rather than a product.
- **Weaknesses:** zero standardisation, no mocks, no reporting, no institutional brand a nervous parent can trust; the £70+/hr tier is London-agency priced without local presence.

### 8. Adnan Khan Tutoring (online into Oxford) & Kip McGrath
- **Adnan Khan Tutoring:** 24+ years, markets an "11 Plus Tuition Oxford" page (live online classes in English, VR, NVR, vocabulary, maths); 4-week summer booster course £599 for 12 sessions ([Adnan Khan Oxford page](https://www.adnankhantutoring.co.uk/11-plus-tuition-oxford/), [summer course](https://www.adnankhantutoring.co.uk/11-plus-summer-booster-course/)). Weakness: High Wycombe-based volume operator; Oxford page is SEO reach, not local presence.
- **Kip McGrath:** Oxford "centre" is actually delivered via Kip McGrath Online; sessions average ~£35–36/hr ([Kip McGrath Oxford](https://www.kipmcgrath.co.uk/oxford), [Kip McGrath pricing](https://www.kipmcgrath.co.uk/pricing)). Weakness: English/maths remediation brand, thin 11+ specialism, no physical Oxford footprint despite the name.

*Also present:* [Oxford Tutors](https://oxfordtutors.com/) (11+/13+ through Oxbridge admissions; premium agency positioning) and Oakley Tutors (Common Entrance/MCS/Oxford High prep, [oakleytutors.com](https://www.oakleytutors.com)) — both bespoke-agency models, no platform, no mocks.

## (c) National chains / big brands

### 9. MyTutor — the 1:1 marketplace benchmark
- **Pricing:** 11+ tutors £22–£55/hr, most £26–£40/hr; pay-as-you-go, no subscription ([MyTutor pricing](https://www.mytutor.co.uk/pricing), [MyTutor 11+ tutors](https://www.mytutor.co.uk/view-tutors/11-plus)).
- **Offer:** vetted university-student tutors, online 1:1.
- **Weaknesses:** tutors are undergraduates, not 11+ specialists; no mock exams, no structured GL curriculum; parents assemble the programme themselves.

### 10. Kumon (adjacent competitor for the same monthly budget)
- **Pricing:** ~£70/month per subject plus ~£35 registration, varying by centre ([Kumon UK media room](https://www.kumon.co.uk/media-room), [Kumon Lowton article](https://lowtonandgolbornenews.co.uk/start-your-child-early-with-kumon-and-help-them-reach-their-full-potential/)).
- **Weaknesses for 11+:** worksheet fluency method; explicitly not 11+/reasoning prep — but it anchors what Oxford parents already pay monthly (£140/month for two subjects) for supplementary education.

### 11. The Exam Coach — online group 11+ specialist (targets Oxford by SEO)
- **Pricing:** weekly live online group workshops £20/session; 5-day intensive courses £299 (marked down from £650); also revision packs, marking services, monthly class passes ([The Exam Coach courses/store](https://www.theexamcoach.tv/courses)).
- **Positioning:** "11 Plus Tuition | 11+ Exam Practice Papers"; runs a dedicated Oxford landing page for grammar and independent entry ([The Exam Coach — Oxford](https://www.theexamcoach.tv/11-plus-tuition/oxford)).
- **Weaknesses:** group-only online workshops; no invigilated mock product; the £650→£299 perpetual-sale pricing reads gimmicky for a premium audience.

## (d) GL-style mock exam providers

### 12. Marie Redmond Tuition — the mock-day gold standard (Bucks/Reading/Slough)
- **Pricing:** in-person GL-style mocks: 1 for £80, 2 for £140, 3 for £195, 4 for £240, 5 for £275, £50 each beyond ([Marie Redmond Bucks mocks](https://marieredmond.co.uk/mock-exams/buckinghamshire-11-plus-mock-exams-gl-assessment-style/)).
- **Offer:** 2h30 exam-hall sittings with pre-recorded audio instructions (exactly like the real Bucks GL test); results within 48 hours including per-question video explanations by Marie Redmond MEd, score vs pass mark, section breakdown, anonymised cohort ranking, and behavioural observations. 17 mock dates across Bucks, Slough/Kendrick, Reading Boys (FSCE) and SW Herts in 2026 ([marieredmond.co.uk](https://marieredmond.co.uk/)).
- **Weaknesses:** **in-person only — explicitly not offered online**; geographically locked to Bucks/Berks venues; capacity-constrained; nothing for independent-school candidates.

### 13. Examberry Mocks — multi-region GL mock days
- **Offer:** GL Assessment-style mock days for Bucks (Amersham), Slough (Langley), Tiffin (Richmond), Sutton (Ewell Castle) and independent schools (Ealing); detailed feedback, expert video explanations for every question, results in five working days via password-protected account, comparative ranking stats, hand-marked creative writing ([mocks.examberry.com](https://mocks.examberry.com/)).
- **Pricing:** not displayed on the landing page (booking flow/phone: 020 3540 4077).
- **Weaknesses:** London/Bucks venue footprint only; five-day turnaround vs Redmond's 48 hours; no online sitting option.

### 14. Susan Daughtrey Education (SDE) + Eleven Plus Exams Mocks + Chuckra
- **SDE:** exam-hall-only mocks for Bucks GL-style, SW Herts, Latymer etc.; £10 supplement for creative-writing versions, £20 cancellation fee; base fees only visible in the booking flow ([susandaughtreyeducation.com mock shop](https://susandaughtreyeducation.com/mock_shop)). All 2026 mocks in physical exam halls — no online product.
- **Eleven Plus Exams (elevenplusexams.co.uk):** 20-year incumbent forum/shop brand running GL/CEM/ISEB-tailored mocks in-person and online, plus published mock books ([mocks.elevenplusexams.co.uk](https://mocks.elevenplusexams.co.uk/), [shop](https://shop.elevenplusexams.co.uk/product-category/11-plus-mock-exams/)).
- **Chuckra 11+ — the price anchor for online mocks:** in-person mock test day £70 inc VAT; **"Virtual Mock" (online, 2 tests, all four subjects) £35 inc VAT; single-subject online "Readiness Mock" £20** ([Chuckra pricing](https://www.11plus.co.uk/pricing/), [virtual mocks](https://www.11plus.co.uk/venues/virtual-mocks/)). Weaknesses: dated brand/UX; virtual mocks are unproctored home sittings; reporting is thin next to Redmond/Examberry.
- **Teachitright:** invigilated mocks in school halls in Gerrards Cross, Maidenhead, Reading, Slough, Windsor (Easter/summer), qualified-teacher invigilation, personal report emailed within three days; open to non-enrolled children ([Teachitright mocks](https://teachitright.com/11-plus-mock-exams/)). Prices in brochure only. No Oxford venue.

## Where the gaps are

**1. Online GL-style mocks with credible conditions and serious reports barely exist.** The providers with the best reports (Marie Redmond: 48h turnaround, per-question video, cohort ranking) are in-person only and say so explicitly; the main online mock (Chuckra Virtual, £35) is an unproctored home sitting with weak reporting. Nobody currently offers **online, invigilated/proctored, audio-instruction GL-style mocks with standardised scores, cohort percentile ranking and video walkthroughs**. That is the single clearest whitespace, and it sits exactly between the £35 online and £80 in-person anchors.

**2. Oxford has no premium local 11+ specialist.** The city's provision is a generalist chain above a Sainsbury's, marketplace tutors averaging £27–32/hr, and out-of-area operators SEO-targeting "11 plus tuition Oxford" (The Exam Coach, Adnan Khan, Kip McGrath online). Meanwhile Owl Tutors charges £70+/hr remotely for MCS prep — proof of premium willingness-to-pay with no local institutional brand capturing it. An Oxford-branded specialist serving both MCS/Oxford High/Abingdon entry *and* commutable Bucks/Reading GL grammars has no direct incumbent.

**3. No mock exam days anywhere near Oxford.** Every credible GL mock-day operator (Redmond, SDE, Examberry, Teachitright) runs venues in Bucks/Berks/London. Oxford-based families sitting Bucks or Reading tests must travel to Amersham, Maidenhead or Slough for a realistic mock. A hybrid model (Oxford venue days + online proctored sittings) would be unique.

**4. The category leader is beatable on calm and trust.** Atom's documented weaknesses — score volatility, timer-induced stress, discouraging difficulty, clunky UX — are the *emotional* core of the 11+ parent's anxiety. A "honest, low-stress, statistically sound" positioning (stable standardised scores, transparent methodology, child-friendly pacing) directly attacks the leader's soft spot rather than its feature list.

**5. Nobody bundles tuition + mocks + reporting into one programme.** Platforms sell practice (Atom/BOFA/CGP), agencies sell hours (MyTutor/Owl), mock companies sell exam days (Redmond/Examberry) — parents stitch together all three, typically spending £2,750–£4,500/yr on tutoring alone ([Atom cost guide](https://www.atomlearning.com/blog/11-plus-tutoring-cost), [TutorLab rates research](https://www.tutorlab.uk/research/private-tutor-rates-uk)). An integrated "programme" (small-group or 1:1 tuition + scheduled GL mock series + longitudinal progress reports) matches how families actually buy and justifies premium pricing.

**6. Independent-school and grammar tracks are served by different players.** Pretest Plus/BOFA own school-specific pretests; Redmond/SDE own GL grammar mocks. Oxford's market uniquely needs both under one roof — a differentiator no national player is structured to deliver.
