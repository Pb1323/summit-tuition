# 11+ Marketing Channels — Research Summary

Last updated: 2026-07-10. Free/low-cost, non-paid-ads channels for reaching parents (with a focus on Indian/Chinese community routes and school-adjacent channels), based on web research.

## Community organisations (Indian/Chinese diaspora)

11+ prep is disproportionately used by Indian and Chinese British families, and both communities have dense, pre-existing weekend-education infrastructure worth approaching directly:

- **Chinese supplementary/Saturday schools** — Mandarin/Cantonese weekend schools already teach English/Maths alongside language classes, so parents are primed for "extra tuition" offers. Examples: Manchester Chinese Centre (500+ pupils), Harrow Chinese School, Coventry Chinese School, Swire Chinese Language Foundation (multiple UK centres), Cambridge Chinese School. Approach: ask to leave flyers, sponsor a newsletter mention, or offer a free diagnostic session for their pupils.
- **Hindu temples / cultural centres** — run community, educational and charitable events and often have noticeboards or WhatsApp broadcast lists for the local Indian community. Examples found: Hindu Temple Cultural & Community Centre of Nottingham, Chinmaya Mission UK (multiple centres, runs children's classes). Worth contacting local temples in your target catchment areas directly.
- **WhatsApp parent groups** — active but many enforce strict "no tutor advertising" rules; direct self-promotion often gets removed. Better approach: join as a genuinely helpful participant (answer questions, share free resources) rather than posting ads — or find the few groups that explicitly allow tutor listings.

## School-adjacent channels

- **Grammar school consortia** — ~25 formal/informal consortia run 11+ admissions regionally (e.g. Kent, Buckinghamshire, CSSE in Surrey/Sussex). In areas like Kent/Bucks, state primary pupils are often auto-registered for the test, meaning nearly every parent at the local primary is a live prospect around Year 5. CSSE already partners with an edtech platform (Atom Learning) for prep — a precedent for schools/consortia endorsing a prep provider.
- **Primary school PTA/newsletter** — direct outreach to Year 4/5 class teachers or PTAs in areas near grammar schools, offering a free parent talk ("What is the 11+ and when should you start?") — a low-pressure way in that doubles as lead generation.
- **Local Facebook parent/area groups** — active but many dedicated "11+ [county]" groups explicitly ban tutor self-promotion; general local parenting/area Facebook groups are more permissive. Mumsnet's tutoring forum was also flagged as a common place tutors mention getting leads, subject to site rules on self-promotion.

## What's realistically actionable without ad spend

| Channel | Effort | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Chinese Saturday schools | Medium (outreach emails/calls) | High-intent audience already paying for weekend supplementary education |
| Hindu temples/cultural centres | Medium (outreach + relationship) | Community trust matters more than a cold pitch — attend an event first |
| Primary school PTA talks | Medium-high (needs a relationship or a warm intro) | Best conversion rate; positions you as expert, not salesperson |
| WhatsApp/Facebook parent groups | Low, but easily backfires | Only works by being a genuinely useful presence, not posting ads |
| Grammar school consortium sites | Low (just check for tutor-directory listings) | Some consortia list approved/recommended providers |

## Recommended next step
Start with 3–5 Chinese supplementary schools and 2–3 Hindu temples/cultural centres within your service area, offer a free diagnostic session or parent talk in exchange for a flyer/newsletter mention — cheapest, highest-trust channel with no ad spend and no platform self-promo restrictions to navigate.
